    Autonomous Ball-Collecting Robot

    Overview

    Under the guidance of Professor Gabriel Hugh Elkaim, our team, including Ana Elizabeth Villa and Rafael Delwart, developed an autonomous robot as part of the final lab in Mechatronics (ECE-118). Tasked with navigating a game field, the robot locates, traps, and deposits 25mm chrome balls either within itself or onto the opponent's field. Our design, "The Roller," utilized tape sensors and bumpers for navigation and employed an efficient ball collection mechanism to meet the challenge of collecting and depositing at least 30 balls within a two-minute round. This project showcased a blend of mechanical design, electrical system integration, and software strategy, culminating in a successful demonstration at the final competition.

    Detailed Report: Autonomous Ball-Collecting Robot

    Technical Details

    Mechanical Design

    The robot's mechanical design focused on efficient ball collection and deposit:

    • Custom-designed chassis optimized for stability and maneuverability
    • Specialized ball collection mechanism with rotating drum
    • Robust bumper system for obstacle detection and navigation
    • Internal storage compartment for collected balls

    Electrical Systems

    The electrical architecture included:

    • PIC32 microcontroller as the central processing unit
    • Tape sensors for line detection and field navigation
    • Bump sensors for obstacle avoidance and perimeter detection
    • Motor control circuits for differential drive and collection mechanism
    • Power management system for optimal battery usage

    Software Implementation

    The control software featured:

    • State machine architecture for robust decision making
    • Sensor fusion algorithms for improved environmental awareness
    • Path planning and obstacle avoidance strategies
    • Game-specific behaviors for efficient ball collection and deposition
